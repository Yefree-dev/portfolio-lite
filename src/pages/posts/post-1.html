Beginning

How to start a new career.

A few years ago, I was working in business administration, following what felt like a “safe” and “logical” career path. But deep down, I knew it wasn’t truly what I wanted. I always admired how technology could solve real-world problems, and I found myself increasingly drawn to how software was shaping the world.

The turning point came when I realized I didn’t want to spend the next decade feeling stuck. I wanted to create, to build, and to solve problems in a different way. That’s when I decided to switch gears entirely and pursue a career in software engineering.

At first, the idea was terrifying. I didn’t have a technical background, and the thought of starting from scratch was overwhelming. But I reminded myself that learning is a skill, and I had already developed resilience and problem-solving from my time in business.

I began by taking online courses, practicing coding every day, and working on small projects. Every bug I fixed and every program I built gave me a small but powerful sense of accomplishment. Slowly but surely, I was building not just technical skills, but also confidence.

Breaking into tech wasn’t easy. It required persistence, rejection, and a lot of self-belief. But my experience in business admin actually gave me an edge, I understood project management, I knew how to communicate with teams, and I was comfortable with structured problem-solving.

Looking back, I’m proud of the risks I took. Switching careers taught me that it’s never too late to follow a different path. If you’re thinking about changing careers, especially into tech, my advice is simple: be patient with yourself, keep showing up every day, and don’t be afraid to be a beginner.

Every expert was once a beginner too.
