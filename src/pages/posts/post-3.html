The Best Resume:

The First Impression Is What Matters

In the tech world — especially as a frontend or web developer — your résumé is often your first line of communication with a company. Before they see your code, your portfolio, or even your LinkedIn, they see that one-page snapshot of who you are.

And like it or not, first impressions matter.

Recruiters spend seconds scanning résumés. That means you don’t need a flashy design or a five-page list of everything you've ever done — you need clarity, relevance, and impact.

Here’s what I’ve learned while building my own résumé in tech:

1. Simplicity Wins

Keep your layout clean. Use clear sections, readable fonts, and white space. Let your content shine — not the template.

2. Tailor It for the Role

Don’t send the same résumé to every job. Read the job description and highlight the skills and projects that match it. If a job mentions React, and you’ve built with React — lead with that.

3. Show Impact, Not Just Duties

Instead of:

Built landing pages for company website

Say:

Developed 3 marketing landing pages using React and Tailwind, increasing lead conversion by 25%

Recruiters want to know what you did and why it mattered.

4. Projects Matter More Than Job Titles

Especially if you’re early in your dev career or switched fields (like I did), your projects tell your story. Link to live demos or GitHub repos. Highlight what tech you used and what problems you solved.

5. Your Résumé Reflects Your Code

Messy, hard-to-read résumé? They’ll assume your code looks the same. Concise, clean, well-organized résumé? You’re already giving the impression of a thoughtful developer.

Final Thought

Your résumé is like the homepage of a website. If it’s cluttered, confusing, or generic, people leave. But if it’s clear, focused, and gives them a reason to stay — you’re in.

So treat your résumé with the same care you’d give a first client meeting, a production deployment, or a homepage redesign. Because that first impression?

It might just be your foot in the door.
