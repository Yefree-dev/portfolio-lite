Consistency

The key to find success.

When I first started learning web development, everything felt overwhelming. HTML, CSS, JavaScript, frameworks, tools — it was like trying to learn a new language while juggling five others. At times, it felt like I wasn’t making any progress at all.

But I kept showing up.

Every day, I coded.

Some days for hours, some days for just 30 minutes, but I stayed consistent.

And that made all the difference.

In web development, it’s easy to compare yourself to others — to see polished portfolios, senior dev titles, or people launching startups and feel like you’re falling behind. But the truth is, progress in this field comes from consistency, not speed.

Here’s what I’ve learned:

  • 1. Code Every Day (Even a Little)

    You don’t have to build a full app every week. Even reading docs, solving one bug, or experimenting with a new CSS trick matters. Small efforts stack up.

  • 2. Repetition Builds Intuition

    When you’re learning something new, it’s easy to get stuck. But the key to success is to keep going. If you’re not consistent, you’ll never get there.

  • 3. Projects > Perfection

    Don’t wait to be “ready” to build something. Just build. Each project, no matter how small, teaches you something new. Over time, you’ll be shocked at how much you’ve grown.

  • 4. Plateaus Are Normal

    Some weeks you feel like a genius. Others, like you forgot everything. It’s normal. Stick with it — consistency turns dips into breakthroughs.

Whether you’re self-taught or coming from a bootcamp or career change (like I did), know this: You don’t need to be the fastest learner or the most talented developer. You just need to be the one who keeps going.

Because in web development — and really, in anything — consistency beats everything.
